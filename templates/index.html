Chemical Process Yield Prediction - JNTUH CHEMICAL

Dataset Overview

This dataset is taken from BASF and simulates a chemical process where the goal is to predict the yield of a product based on various process parameters. It includes 1,000 data points and captures common variables in chemical engineering processes such as temperature, pressure, and catalyst concentration.

The ultimate objective is to build predictive models that can optimize the process conditions and increase the yield, which is crucial in industrial chemical processes like ammonia production via the Haber-Bosch process.

Target Variable

The Yield is the target variable representing the output of the chemical process. It is influenced by multiple parameters and is crucial for optimizing the efficiency of the process.

Input Features

The following process parameters influence the yield:

  • Temperature (°C): Temperature affects reaction rates and product distribution.
  • Pressure (atm): Higher pressure can increase reaction rates and shift equilibria.
  • Catalyst Concentration (mol/L): Catalysts improve the efficiency of reactions without being consumed.
  • Feed Composition (mol fraction): Higher reactant concentration can lead to higher yields.
  • Reaction Time (hours): The duration of the reaction significantly impacts the yield.
  • Reactor Volume (L): Affects residence time and reaction scaling.
  • Cooling Rate (°C/min): Helps in achieving desired product distributions and preventing side reactions.
  • Agitation Speed (RPM): Improves heat and mass transfer, ensuring homogeneity.

Predictive Task

The primary task is to build models to predict the yield of the chemical process based on the given features. This approach can help optimize the process conditions, minimize waste, and improve product quality.

Industry Context

While this dataset is synthetic, it simulates conditions typically encountered in industrial chemical production, such as in companies like BASF, which are involved in processes like ammonia production. These companies frequently leverage data-driven approaches to optimize their chemical processes, reduce costs, and improve sustainability.

Applications

Such predictive models can be valuable in industries like:

  • Petrochemicals
  • Pharmaceuticals
  • Fertilizer Manufacturing
  • Fine Chemicals
